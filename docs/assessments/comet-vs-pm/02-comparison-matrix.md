## Key differences (the ones that actually matter)

1. Scope, not rivalry: Comet is a single-change build ENGINE that bundles OpenSpec+Superpowers under a phase state machine; pm is a multi-epic CONDUCTOR that sits ABOVE any build lane. Comet competes with pm's OpenSpec/Superpowers LANE, not with pm's portfolio role. Adopting Comet would not replace pm — it would replace the lane pm orchestrates.

2. Enforcement model is the sharpest technical divide: Comet HARD-blocks out-of-phase work via evidence guards + a PreToolUse hook; pm is instruction-only by architectural law. Comet gives a mechanical guarantee pm structurally cannot — and pm gives portfolio/detour/memory Comet structurally cannot.

3. Portfolio + detour stack + reconcile gate + Honcho/tracker awareness are pm-unique and are precisely the coordination Comet leaves 'to the human/agent, outside the tool.'

4. Cost of power: Comet buys enforcement, 33-platform reach, and a skill/eval/dashboard platform with 8 deps, a large beta codebase, bilingual upkeep, and brittle downstream coupling to two upstream CLIs. pm buys auditability and sovereignty with a single zero-dep file — at the price of soft, honor-system enforcement.

5. Both independently solved compaction survival with a filesystem-as-system-of-record discipline — the strongest shared idea and the cleanest evidence that they are complementary layers converging on the same durability principle.
